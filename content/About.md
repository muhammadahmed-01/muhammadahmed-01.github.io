I'm Muhammad — a backend engineer based in Lahore with 3 years building and operating distributed systems at Careem, the Middle East's largest ride-hailing platform (Uber's $3.1B acquisition).

I work on the things that matter when systems get serious: reliability under load, data integrity under concurrency, observability when something breaks at 2am, and cost efficiency when the cloud bill arrives.

Most of my best work started with a question I couldn't answer without measuring it. Why did latency spike 18x when we went from 10 to 100 users? Why did the monitoring show all green while $9,640 vanished silently from a ledger? Those questions led to real findings — and the findings are what I write about here.

I work well independently, communicate in writing, and operate across timezones without needing someone to tell me what to do next. I've owned production systems on-call, driven initiatives end-to-end, and shipped things that required aligning engineers, product, and stakeholders across multiple teams.

**Currently at** Careem, owning reliability for 15+ microservices in a multi-region production environment.

**I write about** backend systems, production war stories, and the engineering decisions behind them.
